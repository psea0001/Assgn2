Malaria in Africa: Unraveling the Epidemic

A life-threatening disease spread to humans by some types of mosquitoes.

Why Malaria? Why Africa?

From 2007 to 2017, Malaria remained a dominant health issue in warm, tropical regions worldwide. Yet its long-lasting presence in Africa demands a closer look at the underlying causes. (Prevention, 2020)

    Ecological: Africa's diverse ecology nurtures both malaria-transmitting vectors and the Plasmodium parasite, leading to continuous transmission.
    Governance: Some African regions grapple with political upheaval and frail governance, impeding pivotal health initiatives.
    Socio-economic Challenges: Many African nations face barriers to effective malaria control due to prevailing poverty, underdeveloped healthcare systems, and restricted access to vital services.
